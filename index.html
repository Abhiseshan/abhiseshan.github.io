About ME!

I am a first year undergrad engineering student at the University of Toronto. I did my high school in Dubai, UAE where I was selected to be the IT Coordinator which led to me founding IHS Tech Fest, a yearly event where I held the position of Chairman of the organizing committee. I also played on the school Tennis team.

In my senior year, I served as the Graphics Designer for Velox, the team that built the fastest car in the UAE National Finals in F1 in Schools Competition 2013.

Since starting at UofT, I have joined many clubs and occasionally do some graphics for the engineering comedy newspaper Toike Oike.

At every oppertunity, I head out to different colleges to attend hackathons which are basically 24-36 hour competetions where you come out with a working protoype of an App or hardware product. In the first hackathon I attened, I teamed up with two students from Waterloo and Carleton University and created the award winning home monitoring system Homey.

UoftHacks
Homey

Homey

Project Description

Homey is a home monitoring system which is powered by Intel Edison and can be controlled using Android and Pebble watches. This project won the best use of Intel Technology in UofTHacks 2015. Homey could receive instructions from multiple android devices or pebble devices configured to the Intel Edison. It can measure temperature and display temperature, check if the lights are on detect intruders when you are away, saving electricity and protecting your home while you are away.


  • Designed a Home Monitoring System using Intel Edison Platform
  • Used JavaSctipt collect data from various sensors
  • Designed a companion Pebble Smart Watch App that received the data from sensors using AJAX
  • Effectively used NodeJS with Express Framework to transmit the sensor data to the companion Android and Pebble app
Game Development
Baseball with Iron Man

Baseball with Iron Man

Project Description

Baseball with Iron Man is a Unity Game which uses Myo armband as an input controller. Oculus integration into the system puts you into the batters feet, which perfectly displays the realistic 3D baseball park ecosystem created. Iron Man pitches the balls with random power and speed.


  • Designed in Unity, sketchup and 3DS Max
  • Randomiser and Score scripts written in js and C#
  • Oculus integration
  • Myo Controller for baseball bat
HPVDT
HPVDT

Human Powered Vehicle Design Team

Project Description

The Human-Powered Vehicle Design Team (HPVDT) is a student organization at the University of Toronto that is focused on the design and construction of innovative, high-performance, human-powered vehicles. Our goal is to provide students with practical, hands-on experience in engineering design while promoting efficiency, sustainability and the use of human power as a means of reducing society's impact on the environment.


  • Work with composites, such as carbon fibre, and other advanced materials
  • Tasked with designing the handle bars for the 2015 vehicle
  • Building the vehicle

For more information, visit HPVDT Website
